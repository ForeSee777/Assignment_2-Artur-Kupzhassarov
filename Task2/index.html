About Einstein

Albert Einstein (1879–1955) was a German-born theoretical physicist who developed the theory of relativity, one of the two pillars of modern physics. He received the Nobel Prize in Physics in 1921 for his explanation of the photoelectric effect. His work transformed our understanding of space, time, and energy.

1879 – 1955

Major Life Events

  1. 1905 – Publishes the Annus Mirabilis papers.
  2. 1921 – Wins the Nobel Prize in Physics.
  3. 1933 – Moves to the United States, joins Princeton.
  4. 1939 – Warns Roosevelt about nuclear weapons.
  5. 1955 – Dies in Princeton, New Jersey.

Key Contributions

  • Theory of Relativity
  • Photoelectric Effect
  • E = mc²
  • Brownian Motion Explanation
